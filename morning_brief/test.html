Editor’s Highlights
Nearly $10 Billion in COVID Relief Benefits Paid to Ineligible Applicants: CRA

The CRA says nearly $10 billion in COVID payments went out to individuals who weren’t entitled to them. The actual figure will be higher since it’s just an estimate based on audits and doesn’t include businesses.
The agency has recovered less than 20 percent.
Why It Matters: We’re still tallying the cost of the pandemic, and how taxpayers’ money was spent is an important measure of the government’s overall response.

Winnipeg Lab Under Scrutiny as US Lawmakers Seek Briefing on CCP Infiltration

A U.S. Congressional committee investigating the origins of the COVID virus wants to hear what the U.S. intelligence community knows about two Beijing-linked scientists at Canada’s top biosafety lab.
The committee has asked the U.S. director of national intelligence for a briefing about the Winnipeg lab and findings by the Canadian Security Intelligence Service.
Why It Matters: As the U.S. investigates the pandemic’s origins, it’s eyeing China’s involvement, including its infiltration of Canada.
